About Me

My Journey

I am a PhD candidate in Biomedical Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology and Emory University, working in the Madabhushi Lab. My research focuses on developing clinically grounded machine learning methods for medical imaging, spanning computational pathology and radiology.

I build image-based biomarkers for diagnosis, therapy response and survival prediction, with models validated on multi-institutional, clinical trial cohorts to support translational oncology. My work has been published in Nature Reviews Clinical Oncology and the Journal of Bone and Mineral Research Plus.

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a Radiology Imaging Fellow at the Indian Institute of Science, developing 3D ultrasound reconstruction for underserved communities. I hold an MEng from the University of Cincinnati and a BE from M.S. Ramaiah Institute of Technology.

Research Interests

🧬 Computational Pathology

Developing image-based biomarkers from digitized histopathology slides for diagnosis, therapy response prediction, and survival analysis in oncology.

🩺 Translational AI for Oncology

Building and validating machine learning models on multi-institutional clinical trial data (NSABP, ECOG, SWOG) to support clinical decision-making in cancer care.

🔧 Medical Image Analysis

Designing deep learning pipelines for automated segmentation, morphological feature extraction, and 3D image reconstruction across radiology and pathology.
