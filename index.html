About

I am a PhD candidate in Biomedical Engineering at Georgia Tech and Emory University, part of Madabhushi Lab. My research focuses on developing clinically grounded machine learning methods for medical imaging, spanning computational pathology and radiology. I build image-based biomarkers for diagnosis, therapy response and survival prediction, with models validated on multi-institutional, clinical trial cohorts to support translational oncology.

Research

Machine Learning | Computational Pathology

Image-Based Biomarkers for HER2+ Breast Cancer (Clinical Cancer Research)

Integrating clinical trial data with computational pathology to identify spatial immune biomarkers that predict response to trastuzumab-based regimens and support treatment personalization in HER2+ breast cancer.

Artificial Intelligence in the Medical Domain (Nature Reviews Clinical Oncology)

Co-authored high-impact reviews examining the promises, pitfalls, and clinical realities of AI in digital pathology and oncology, including a perspective in Nature Reviews Clinical Oncology.

Read more Deep Learning | Computational Pathology

Segmentation in Bone Biopsy Images and Bone Turnover Classification (Journal of Bone and Mineral Research)

Built a deep-learning pipeline for automated segmentation and morphological texture analysis of undecalcified bone biopsy images to classify bone turnover status relevant to osteoporosis management.

Read more 3D Image Reconstruction | Computational Radiology

3D Display-Less Ultrasound for Prenatal Screening (SPIE)

Developed algorithms for 3D fetal volume reconstruction from 2D ultrasound and designed scanning protocols for healthcare workers with limited experience, bringing prenatal imaging to underserved communities in India.

Read more Image Processing | Computational Radiology

Ground Penetrating Radar for Disaster Relief

Evaluated portable RADAR scanning as an alternative to X-ray for identifying severe pelvic fractures at disaster sites, generating 3D pelvic models from RADAR imagery.

Read more Image Processing | Computational Radiology

Fetal Cardiac Chamber Detection

Developed an algorithm to segment and quantify fetal heart chambers from ultrasound, evaluating ventricular ratios to detect cardiac malformations in-utero.
